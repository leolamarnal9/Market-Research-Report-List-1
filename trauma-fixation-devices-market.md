<p><p>The trauma fixation devices market is categorized into internal and external fixation devices. Internal fixation devices, such as plates, screws, and intramedullary nails, are used to stabilize broken bones internally, allowing for better mobility and faster healing. External fixation devices, on the other hand, consist of rods and frames that are placed outside the body to support and align fractured bones. Both types are essential in orthopedic surgeries, addressing various injuries and ensuring proper healing for patients.</p></p>
