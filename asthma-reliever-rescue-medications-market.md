<p><p>Asthma reliever medications, often referred to as rescue medications, primarily fall into two categories: quick-relief and long-term control medications. Quick-relief medications, such as short-acting beta-agonists, provide immediate symptom relief during an asthma attack, easing breathing swiftly. Long-term control medications, including inhaled corticosteroids, are used daily to stabilize asthma, reducing frequency and severity of attacks by managing underlying inflammation. Both types are essential for effective asthma management, ensuring patients can address immediate symptoms while maintaining overall lung health.</p></p>
